# Book Overview

Welcome to the AI-native Textbook for Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics.

This textbook is designed to provide a comprehensive and hands-on understanding of the rapidly evolving fields of Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics. It aims to bridge the gap between theoretical concepts and practical applications, leveraging the power of AI-driven development.

## Purpose and Target Audience

The primary purpose of this textbook is to serve as a foundational resource for students, researchers, and professionals interested in the design, development, and deployment of intelligent robots. The target audience ranges from undergraduate and graduate students in robotics, AI, and computer science to engineers and researchers working in the industry.

## Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this textbook, readers will be able to:
*   Understand the core components of robotic systems, including perception, cognition, and action.
*   Develop and integrate robotic software using ROS 2.
*   Utilize digital twin technologies (Gazebo, Unity) for simulation and testing.
*   Explore AI-robot brain technologies, particularly with NVIDIA Isaac.
*   Grasp the concepts of Vision-Language-Action (VLA) for advanced robot control.
*   Apply AI-native development principles to robotics projects.

## Prerequisites

Readers should have a basic understanding of:
*   Programming concepts (preferably Python).
*   Linear algebra and calculus.
*   Basic physics and mechanics.

This textbook will provide necessary introductions to specialized topics, but foundational knowledge will enhance the learning experience.
